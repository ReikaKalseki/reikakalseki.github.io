General

Aqueous Engineering adds several new machines and devices for Seabases, adding convenience tools, automation machinery, and other infrastructure. It also greatly expands on ACUs, making them more visually interesting and more useful.

It also adds outdoor versions of the small plant pots for convenience and decoration purposes.

Machines

Seabase Sonar Antenna

Continuously fires sonar pulses from a seabase.

Creature Repellant Pylon

Discourages close approach of creatures to the seabase.

Seabase Beacon

A beacon affixed to the seabase that broadcasts location and status information.

Ampeel Antenna

Draws small amounts of power from passing ampeels to maintain power in a seabase. Most effective at short range. Not capable of sustaining high power loads.

Autofarming Unit

Automatically harvests from mature plants in nearby growbeds. Will not completely kill the plant.

ACU Cleaning System

Keeps the ACU clean of debris, including excreted waste from the contained animals.

Scanner Camera Relay Antenna

Eliminates the distance attenuation from scanner room camera signals for the attached seabase.

Seabase Battery Bank

Generates no power, but significantly increases the maximum storage of a seabase, allowing it to last longer between intermittent power cycles. Stackable effect.

ACU Feeding System

Periodically adds small amounts of plankton to the ACU to top up food supplies for herbivorous fish, increasing their fertility. Only available if Ecocean is installed, as it uses its plankton as a "fuel".

Plankton Feeder

Disperses plankton into the water to attract filter feeders and help encourage their reproduction in a wild setting. Only available if Ecocean is installed, as it uses its plankton as a "fuel".


ACU Expansions

Byproducts

Larger creatures in ACUs now serve a purpose beyond decoration; they will consume food if it is available and generate small quantities of waste. This waste is like a smaller version of that which is obtained from Sea Treaders, and can be used as bioreactor fuel, and as a crafting ingredient to make the larger item for other mods' crafting recipes.
What happens when it is left to accumulate.

There are also other items that can be situationally obtained from specific species. For example, if stalkers in an ACU have access to shiny metal (scrap, titanium, or silver, with the last giving the highest yields) dropped inside, they will also drop stalker teeth, resulting in the ability to "farm" them.

Overall, this mechanic pairs well with the ACU cleaning system.

Ecosystems

AE adds an "ecosystem" mechanic to ACUs. This means that building consistent and/or healthy ecosystems will boost productivity, meaning increased breeding rates, increased byproduct generation rates, and more. Here, 'healthy' is defined as containing a complete and balanced food chain and appropriate creature density, following a few specific rules:
  • At least one plant
  • At least one herbivore
  • At least one carnivore
  • At most four herbivore "units" per plant
  • At least six herbivore "units" per carnivore
  • At most 1.5 carnivores per ACU in the stack
Sparkle ("Enzyme Host") peepers further boost ecosystem health, both as a flat bonus per peeper, as well as adding more flexibility to the limits above.

"Herbivore Units" is a reference to the fact that some larger herbivores count as equivalent to several smaller fish for the purposes of this calculation.

Biomes And Visuals

If all the species - plants, small creatures, and large creatures - share one and only one native biome, the ACU will take on a "theme", and change its appearance to emulate that biome's environment. This change is not purely aesthetic; consistent-theme ACUs further increase the bonuses described above. Sparkle peepers and cuddlefish are counted as valid for any theme, and will not affect selection, even for biomes where they do not naturally occur.
Several of the different biome themes in ACUs. Kelp is visible above.

Note that the biome list is slightly simplified to be more "biome archetypes" than literal game-defined biomes. While things like the shallows, kelp forest, jellyshroom cave, lost river, or grand reef have direct counterparts, less "ecologically unique" biomes like the crag, crash zone, dunes, and underwater islands are lumped into "other".
