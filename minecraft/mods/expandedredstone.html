Overview

Expanded Redstone adds a few machines and tools that make redstone more useful and powerful. They are designed to "feel" like vanilla, but work just as well in a heavily modded environment. This is NOT meant to be a replacement for things like RedPower, and will not have things like new wires, mining machines, and other things better placed in a true tech mod.

The machines can be reoriented by right-clicking them with a BuildCraft/OmniTools wrench or a RotaryCraft screwdriver, or simply breaking and placing them again.
Modes are switched by right-clicking the block with an empty hand or item that has no special effect.

Machines

  • BUD: Detects changes in front of it, and sends a signal pulse out the back if it finds a change.
  • Camouflage Block: Takes on the texture and hitbox of whatever is below it if it is powered.
  • Item Effector: Mimics the right-click actions of items.
  • Block Breaker: Breaks blocks in front of it and drops them as items behind it. It has 4 harvest levels:
  • Diamond: Breaks up to 12 blocks, anything breakable (except lapis blocks)
  • Iron: Breaks 1 block, anything breakable but obsidian
  • Stone: Breaks 1 block, only blocks harvestable by hand
  • Wood: Same as stone, but only lasts 128 uses
  • Block Placer: Places a block from its inventory in front of it.
  • Signal Driver: Sends a signal of a strength you select.
  • Chest Reader: Will output a signal if its chest is full or empty, depending on the setting.
  • Redstone Clock: Will send a signal at intervals controlled by one of many preset settings.
  • Proximity Detector: Send a signal if mobs or players (depending on selected mode) are within 16 blocks.
  • Toggle Latch: Functions like any T latch. Send it a signal, it turns on; send another, it turns off, and so on.
  • Weather Sensor: Emits a signal if it is raining or storming.
  • Signal Emitter: Sends out the wireless redstone beam.
  • Signal Receiver: Reads the wireless redstone beam and outputs power if applicable.
  • Redstone Pump: Sucks up one block of fluid per positive redstone edge.
  • Hopper Ticker: Makes a hopper transfer an item per positive redstone edge.
  • Signal Scaler: Scales one redstone signal range (0-15) into a smaller range for ease of control.
  • Column Decrementer: Acts somewhat like vertical redstone.
  • Wireless analog transmitter/receiver: Multichannel wireless analog redstone transmission.
  • Bus Latch: A 16-channel RS latch, designed for use with PR bundled cable or MFR rednet
